The CES-D (Center for Epidemiologic Studies Depression Scale) is a self-report depression screening tool developed by the U.S. National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH). It has been widely used in epidemiological studies on depression in community populations. In a study with healthy older adults, the use of theHertzion device showed an approximate 33% reduction in CES-D scores, suggesting a potential positive effect on emotional stability and depressive symptom reduction in the elderly.
